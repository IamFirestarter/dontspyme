PGP – it's open!

PG... What?

PGP stands for Pretty Good Privacy and that is exactly what it does: It protects your privacy pretty good. To use it you generate a set of keys – a private key that you keep on your devices to decrypt mail sent to you and a public one that anybody can use to send you encrypted email.

PGP is completely open source and free to use. There are a lot of email clients and plugins for email clients that you can use. But be aware: Services can still track who you send emails and what the subjects where – so you probably want to use an empty or very general subject.

S/MIME – it's spreaded

S/MIME? What's that?

S/MIME means that you encrypt your email using SSL – the same technology that is used on secured websites. To do so you will need a certificate. You will need to buy this certificate from a so called certifcate authority but there are some who will give you one for free. Also there is a open source authority called CACert.org but to use them you will need to install a so called root certificate on your device.

S/MIME is wide spreaded and supported by almost any email client – even on your mobile phone. But like PGP it will not encrypt subjects and meta data can be collected.
